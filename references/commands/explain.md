# `explain`

An on-demand explanatory command: the candidate names a term, a concept, or
a piece of exam wording they find confusing, and `explain` translates it
into plain language they can actually reason from. Where `learn` decides
*when* an analogy is needed as part of driving the teaching loop, `explain`
exists for the moments the candidate decides that for themselves — mid-quiz
confusion about a distractor's wording, a `resources.md` reading that used
a term without defining it, or simply "I keep seeing this word and don't
actually know what it means." `explain` runs independently of any specific
plan week or session state; it can be invoked on any exam term at any point
in the candidate's preparation.

`explain` never invents its own way of building an analogy. Every response
this command produces is generated by calling `analogy-engine`'s
`analogize(concept, exam_terms)` and returning exactly the three-part
structure that engine defines — see `references/engines/analogy-engine.md`
for the contract, the two failure-point categories, and the worked example
this command relies on rather than restates. `explain` is
certification-agnostic: it builds a fresh everyday-operations comparison for
whatever term or concept is in front of it, with no built-in library of
domain-specific comparisons baked into the command itself.

---

## Behavior

1. **Identify the concept and its exact exam terminology.** The candidate
   may name a single term ("what does 'least privilege' mean"), paste a
   confusing sentence from a resource or a question, or ask about a
   distinction between two terms they keep mixing up ("what's the actual
   difference between authentication and authorization"). `explain`
   resolves this to the precise concept(s) and the exact exam vocabulary
   involved — cross-checking against `exam-facts.md`'s syllabus objectives
   and terminology where the term appears there, so the restatement in
   step 3 below uses the same wording the exam itself uses, not a loose
   paraphrase.
2. **Call `analogy-engine`'s `analogize(concept, exam_terms)`.** `explain`
   passes the resolved concept and its exact exam terms into the engine and
   receives back the mandatory three-part structure. `explain` does not
   trim, reorder, or make any of the three parts optional — a response
   missing the caveat is not a valid `explain` answer, per the engine's own
   contract.
3. **Deliver all three parts in order.**
   - **The everyday-operations analogy** — the plain-language comparison,
     presented first so the candidate has something concrete to hold onto
     before the technical vocabulary is reintroduced.
   - **The precise technical restatement** — every element named in the
     analogy mapped one to one back to the exact exam terminology, so the
     candidate can read the same structure named twice: once in plain
     language, once in the vocabulary the exam actually uses.
   - **Where this analogy breaks down** — the named limits on the
     comparison, stated as concretely as the analogy itself (structural
     mismatches, behavioral mismatches under stress) so the candidate knows
     exactly where to stop reasoning from the everyday picture and switch
     back to the technical model.
4. **Offer a check.** After delivering the three parts, `explain` invites
   the candidate to try restating the concept in their own words or to
   apply it to a quick scenario, so a misunderstanding introduced by an
   imperfect first reading of the analogy surfaces immediately rather than
   silently persisting until it costs a `learn` or `quiz` question later.

`explain` is always labeled as an explanatory aid the moment it responds —
the analogy and restatement are generated teaching content, never phrased
as, or mistaken for, official exam wording.

---

## Outputs

`explain` is a conversational, in-session command — it does not write a new
file to `.certicoach/` of its own. It writes nothing to `plan.md` or
`mistake-ledger.md` on its own initiative, since a candidate asking for an
explanation is not the same signal as an answer classified through
`assessment-engine`; if the candidate's follow-up check in step 4 reveals a
genuine misconception, that is `learn`'s (or `quiz`'s) classification loop
to record, not `explain`'s.

Any exam-fact claim `explain` surfaces alongside a concept — for example,
"this term maps to Domain 2, which the guide weights at 25%" — is not part
of the analogy itself and must remain independently sourced through
`provenance-engine`'s labeling rule rather than riding along inside the
generated explanation: pulled from `exam-facts.md` with its existing
`[OFFICIAL — source + date]` tag intact, or, if no such sourced fact exists
yet, left unstated rather than asserted from memory. The analogy and
restatement are always `[GENERATED PRACTICE]`-tier explanatory content; any
factual claim about the exam woven alongside them keeps its own separate,
correctly sourced tier.
